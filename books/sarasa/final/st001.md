## Story N: Snow Bear

Once upon a time, in a land of snowy white, there lived a little bear named Noah. He was kind, gentle, and always honest with everyone he met. The weather that day was windy, and gusts made snowflakes dance across his face like tiny white butterflies.

One windy afternoon, while out for a walk, Noah discovered something exciting. A friendly contest was being held in the meadow beyond the hill! All the animals from the Arctic were invited to play games and share laughter together. Noah's heart filled with excitement. He wanted to join them more than anything!

But when Noah reached the meadow, he found a tall gate blocking his way. The gate was locked tight, like a stubborn puzzle that refused to be solved. Noah wondered why anyone would lock a gate in the middle of the snowy field. He tried pushing it with his paws, but it wouldn't move. He tried using his sharp teeth on the lock, but only made it wobble without opening.

"I really want to join the contest," Noah sighed, feeling a little sad. "But what can I do?"

Just then, Happy Dolphin appeared nearby. He had swum far up a warm stream that flowed from the big ocean into the snowy land. The warm water made the ice melt in that one special spot, creating a cozy pond where Happy Dolphin could visit the meadow! The stream was so warm and cozy that plants and flowers grew there all year round, even in winter.

Happy Dolphin was a friendly dolphin with big smiley eyes, and his tail flicked through the air like it was dancing in the wind. He had visited this magical place many times before, so he knew all its secrets.

"Hello, little Bear!" said Happy Dolphin cheerfully. "I see you're having trouble with the gate."

"Yes," said Noah honestly. "I want to join the contest, but I can't get through."

Happy Dolphin smiled warmly. "This gate is special. Long ago, the animals made it to protect the warm pond where I can visit. It only opens when someone shows patience and calmness — not by pushing or pulling hard. Here, take this silver bell. When the time is right, ring it gently near the flower on the other side of the gate."

Noah held the silver bell in his paws. It was shiny and beautiful, and it felt cool against his fur.

"But how will this help me?" asked Noah.

"Stay calm and wait," said Happy Dolphin with a wink. "Trust yourself. The flower near the gate is magical. It knows when someone is truly patient. I've seen it glow before — but only when someone rings the bell with a quiet heart."

Then Happy Dolphin swam back toward the warm stream, waving his fin goodbye. "Good luck, little Bear!"

Noah took a deep breath to calm his excited heart. The wind blew strongly, making the snow swirl around him. He sat down beside the locked gate and waited patiently. He thought about all the fun games he could play in the contest. He thought about making snow angels and sliding on the ice. Most importantly, he thought about being patient, just like Happy Dolphin had said.

The wind whispered through the trees, and the branches swayed like dancing arms. Noah held the silver bell close to his chest, feeling its coolness turn warm from his body heat. He tilted his head a little higher, took another deep breath, and rang the silver bell softly near the flower.

The sound echoed through the icy air. Suddenly, the nearby flower began to glow! It was like a soft spark in the night sky, growing brighter and brighter. The glow shone through the trees, and Noah saw that the flower was connected to the gate by tiny golden roots that glowed beneath the snow. The magical flower heard the bell and sent its light through the roots, and suddenly, the locked gate swung open!

"Wow!" Noah said happily. "The flower heard me!"

Noah stayed calm and walked through the open gate. He had solved the puzzle by being patient! The meadow was beautiful, with ducks quacking and birds singing. All the animals cheered when they saw him arrive.

Noah joined the friendly contest. He played games with his new friends and laughed until his belly hurt. He was good at making things with his paws — his mama bear had taught him how to shape snow and ice into fun shapes back home. When the snow sculpture contest started, Noah used his gentle paws to carve the most beautiful ice bear the animals had ever seen! Everyone clapped and cheered for him. It was the best day ever!

As the sun began to set, casting golden rays across the snowy land, the magical flower slowly stopped glowing and went back to sleep, its golden roots fading back into the snow. Noah knew he had found something special. The animals welcomed him, and he decided to stay in the warm spot near the water where the flowers and trees bloomed brightly. It was his new home — right there near the cozy pond where Happy Dolphin could visit him anytime!

The night was so still that you could hear your own thoughts. Noah walked through the snow, holding the silver bell tight in his paw. He had earned it for being patient, and he wanted to keep it close. The bell was warm now, just like his happy heart. He stopped by a large tree and sat down, feeling tired but happy inside. He closed his eyes with a soft smile on his face.

Lesson: Patience brings good results.
