## Story N: Snow Bear

Once upon a time, in a land of snowy white, there lived a little bear named Noah. He was kind, gentle, and always honest with everyone he met. The weather that day was windy, and gusts made snowflakes dance across his face like tiny white butterflies.

One windy afternoon, while out for a walk, Noah discovered something exciting. A friendly contest was being held in the meadow beyond the hill! All the animals from the Arctic were invited to play games and share laughter together. Noah's heart filled with excitement. He wanted to join them more than anything!

But when Noah reached the meadow, he found a tall gate blocking his way. The gate was locked tight, like a stubborn puzzle that refused to be solved. Noah tried pushing it with his paws, but it wouldn't move. He tried using his sharp teeth on the lock, but only made it wobble without opening.

"I really want to join the contest," Noah sighed, feeling a little sad. "But what can I do?"

Just then, Happy Dolphin appeared nearby. He was a friendly dolphin with big smiley eyes, and his tail flicked through the air like it was dancing in the wind.

"Hello, little Bear!" said Happy Dolphin cheerfully. "I see you're having trouble with the gate."

"Yes," said Noah honestly. "I want to join the contest, but I can't get through."

Happy Dolphin smiled warmly. "Be patient, little Bear. Stay calm, and something wonderful will happen. Here, take this silver bell. When the time is right, ring it gently."

Noah held the silver bell in his paws. It was shiny and beautiful, and it felt cool against his fur.

"But how will this help me?" asked Noah.

"Stay calm and wait," said Happy Dolphin with a wink. "Trust yourself."

Then Happy Dolphin swam away happily, leaving Noah alone with the bell.

Noah took a deep breath to calm his excited heart. The wind blew strongly, making the snow swirl around him. He sat down beside the locked gate and waited patiently. He thought about all the fun games he could play in the contest. He thought about making snow angels and sliding on the ice. Most importantly, he thought about being patient, just like Happy Dolphin had said.

The wind whispered through the trees, and the branches swayed like dancing arms. Noah held the silver bell close to his chest, feeling its coolness against his skin. He tilted his head a little higher, took another deep breath, and rang the silver bell softly.

The sound echoed through the icy air. Suddenly, a nearby flower began to glow! It was like a soft spark in the night sky, growing brighter and brighter. The glow shone through the trees, and suddenly, the locked gate swung open!

"Wow!" Noah said happily. "The flower heard me!"

Noah stayed calm and walked through the open gate. He had solved the puzzle by being patient! The meadow was beautiful, with ducks quacking and birds singing. All the animals cheered when they saw him arrive.

Noah joined the friendly contest. He played games with his new friends and laughed until his belly hurt. It was the best day ever!

As the sun began to set, casting golden rays across the snowy land, Noah knew he had found something special. The animals welcomed him, and he decided to stay in the warm spot near the water where the flowers and trees bloomed brightly. It was his new home.

The night was so still that you could hear your own thoughts. Noah walked through the snow, the silver bell warm in his hand. He stopped by a large tree and sat down, feeling tired but happy inside. He closed his eyes with a soft smile on his face.

Lesson: Patience brings good results.
