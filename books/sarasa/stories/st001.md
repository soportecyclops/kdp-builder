## Story N: SnowBear Once upon a time, in a land of snowy white, there lived a little bear named Noah. He was kind and gentle, always smiling at the animals he met. The weather that day was windy as a gusty wind made snowflakes dance across his face. One windy afternoon, while out for a walk, Noah found himself near a gate that wouldn’t open. The door locked tight like a puzzle, blocking him from playing in the nearby meadow where ducks quacked and birds sang. Noah felt excited but couldn’t help trying to figure out how to unlock it. He tried using his sharp teeth on the lock, but only made it worse. Then he asked for help from Happy Dolphin who lived nearby.

Noah kept thinking about his meadow friends and how much fun it was to play. His heart felt a little sad when he saw the locked gate. He knew he would have to be patient, maybe find another way to join them.

He walked back home, thinking of all the things he could do instead. Noah thought about making snow angels or drawing pictures on a frozen pond. Finally, his patience paid off as he remembered something helpful - the silver bell! He took out the silver bell from inside his cozy cave and rang it softly.

Noah's heart felt a little excited when he thought about his meadow friends. He remembered how much fun it was to play with them and wondered what games they might be playing now. The wind blew stronger, making the snowflakes dance in the sky. It made Noah feel even more playful inside. He took out his silver bell from inside his cozy cave where he kept all his treasures safe. With a soft touch on its shiny surface, Noah rang it gently. The silver bell echoed softly through the icy air. "Maybe I can ring this bell to see if anyone hears me," thought Noah as he looked around. He imagined his meadow friends listening and laughing at him from far away. But no sound replied.

Happy Dolphin, a friendly dolphin with big smiley eyes, walked/flew into the scene. His tail flicked through the air like it was dancing in the wind. He had one piece of advice for Noah: "Be patient, little Bear." Then he gave Noah his silver bell and flew away happily.

Noah took a deep breath to calm his excited heart. He stared at the locked gate, then back at his trusty silver bell. The wind whispered through the trees, making the branches sway and leaves dance in the air. Noah held the bell close to his chest, feeling its coolness against his skin. "I’m going to ring it one more time," he thought. He tilted his head a little higher, took another deep breath, and rang the silver bell softly. The sound echoed through the icy air again. This time, Noah heard something from far away in the distance. It sounded like laughter, but fainter than before. His eyes widened with hope. "Did they hear me?" he whispered, his voice barely audible.

The wind blew stronger now, making the branches dance more wildly. Noah held his silver bell tight against his chest. He tilted his head up higher and took another deep breath. Then he rang it softly again. This time, a gentle glow appeared on a nearby flower. It was like a soft spark in the night sky. The glow grew brighter, shining through the trees. "Wow! They heard me!" Noah said happily.

Lesson: Patience brings good results.

The night was so still that you could hear your own thoughts as Noah walked through the snow. The silver bell felt warm in his hand. He stopped by a large tree and sat down, closing his eyes. After a moment, he opened them again to see the glowing flower close its petals, as if saying goodbye. Noah smiled softly.

He stood up slowly, feeling a little tired but happy inside. The sun began to peek over the horizon, casting golden rays across the snowy land. A new home was waiting for him in that warm spot near the water where the flowers and trees were always blooming brightly.
